Vocabulary

Jan 4

congruent
similar to or in agreement with something, so that the two things can both exist or can be combined without problems
Our goals are congruent; there is no conflict.
We need the freedom to direct funds in a way that is congruent with local priorities.
endurance
the ability to keep doing something difficult, unpleasant, or painful for a long time
Running a marathon is a test of human endurance.
The pain was bad beyond endurance.
flee
to escape by running away, especially because of danger or fear
She fled (from) the room in tears.
In order to escape capture, he fled to the mountains.

Jan 3

grist for the mill
anything that can be used to your advantage
I might as well learn another language, it’s all grist to the mill when it comes to getting a job.
The most mundane experiences are grist for the mill.
arousal
to cause someone to have a particular feeling
It’s a subject that has aroused a lot of interest.
Our suspicions were first aroused when we heard a muffled scream.
oblivion
the state of being completely forgotten / the state of being completely destroyed
He was another minor poet who was consigned to oblivion.
The planes bombed the city into oblivion.

Jan 2

curator
a person in charge of a museum, library, etc.
She visited the various museums and private collections of this country, and got in touch with the heads of the different departments and their curators.
caveat
a warning to consider something before taking any more action, or a statement that limits a more general statement.
He agreed to the interview, with the caveat that he could approve the final article.
fierce
physically violent and frightening / strong and powerful / showing strong feeling or energetic activity
Two men were shot during fierce fighting last weekend.
Fierce winds prevented the race from taking place. The expansion plans will face fierce opposition/resistance from environmentalists.

Jan 1

distort
to change something from its usual, original, natural, or intended meaning, condition, or shape. Another one is the misinformation effect, a memory bias in which we subconsciously incorporate new information to distort memory of the original event.
My original statement has been completely distorted by the media.
impede
to make it more difficult for something to happen or more difficult for someone to do something.
Together, the cumulative implications of these biases impede our ability to form the accurate and complete picture needed for effective reflection. Although he’s shy, it certainly hasn’t impeded his career in any way.
disposal
he act of getting rid of something, especially by throwing it away.
We are all investors; the currency at our disposal is time and energy.
