Vocabulary

Jan 10

sparse
small in numbers or amount, often spread over a large area.
Information coming out of the disaster area is sparse.
relegate
to put someone or something into a lower or less important rank or position
She resigned when she was relegated to a desk job.
The story was relegated to the middle pages of the paper.
revere
to very much respect and admire someone or something.
Nelson Mandela is revered for his brave fight against apartheid.

Jan 9

laid-back
relaxed in manner and character; not usually worried about other people’s behaviour or things that need to be done
I’ve never seen her worried or anxious in any way - she’s so laid-back.
bevy
a large group of people, especially women or girls, or a large group of similar things
Victorian postcards often featured bevies of bathing beauties.
clairvoyance
a person who says they have powers to see the future or see things that other people cannot see
She went to see a clairvoyant who said he could communicate with her dead husband.

Jan 8

succumb
to lose the determination to oppose something; to accept defeat / to die or suffer badly from an illness
The town finally succumbed last week after being pounded with heavy artillery for more than two months.
I’m afraid I succumbed to temptation and had a piece of cheesecake.
I felt sure it would only be a matter of time before he succumbed to my charms.
Thousands of cows have succumbed to the disease in the past few months.
splurge
to spend a lot of money on buying goods, especially expensive goods
I feel like splurging (out) on a new dress.
disguise
to give a new appearance to a person or thing, especially in order to hide its true form
He disguised himself by wearing a false beard.
Minor skin imperfections can usually be disguised with a spot of make-up.
I couldn’t disguise my disappointment.

Jan 7

frugal
careful when using money or food, or (of a meal) cheap or small in amount
We had very little money, so we ate frugally in cheap caf√©s.
I became more frugal with money and would only buy what I needed.
remedy
a successful way of curing an illness or dealing with a problem or difficulty.
The best remedy for grief is hard work.
You can buy most cold remedies over the counter.
raving
complete or extreme, or completely or extremely.
He must be a raving idiot/lunatic.
She’s no raving beauty.

Jan 6

diligent
careful and using a lot of effort
Leo is very diligent in/about his work.
Cultivate a diligent approach towards ruthless prioritization
Their lawyer was extremely diligent in preparing their case.
detour
a different or less direct route to a place that is used to avoid a problem or to visit somewhere or do something on the way
You’d be wise to make (us also take) a detour to avoid the roadworks.
Traffic detours will be kept to a minimum throughout the festival.
swamp
very wet, soft land
The Everglades are an area of swamp in southern Florida.

Jan 5

loom
to appear as a large, often frightening or unclear shape or object
Dark storm clouds loomed on the horizon. Her final exams are looming.
Here, too, the threat of unemployment has been looming on the horizon.
looming
(of something unwanted or unpleasant) about to happen soon and causing worry
There is the looming issue of job loss due to automation.
gratitude
the feeling or quality of being grateful
She sent them a present to show/express her gratitude.
drain
If you drain something, you remove the liquid from it, usually by pouring it away or allowing it to flow away, and if something drains, liquid flows away or out of it.
Drain the pasta thoroughly.
Don’t bother drying the pans - just leave them to drain.

Jan 4

congruent
similar to or in agreement with something, so that the two things can both exist or can be combined without problems
Our goals are congruent; there is no conflict.
We need the freedom to direct funds in a way that is congruent with local priorities.
endurance
the ability to keep doing something difficult, unpleasant, or painful for a long time
Running a marathon is a test of human endurance.
The pain was bad beyond endurance.
flee
to escape by running away, especially because of danger or fear
She fled (from) the room in tears.
In order to escape capture, he fled to the mountains.

Jan 3

grist for the mill
anything that can be used to your advantage
I might as well learn another language, it’s all grist to the mill when it comes to getting a job.
The most mundane experiences are grist for the mill.
arousal
to cause someone to have a particular feeling
It’s a subject that has aroused a lot of interest.
Our suspicions were first aroused when we heard a muffled scream.
oblivion
the state of being completely forgotten / the state of being completely destroyed
He was another minor poet who was consigned to oblivion.
The planes bombed the city into oblivion.

Jan 2

curator
a person in charge of a museum, library, etc.
She visited the various museums and private collections of this country, and got in touch with the heads of the different departments and their curators.
caveat
a warning to consider something before taking any more action, or a statement that limits a more general statement.
He agreed to the interview, with the caveat that he could approve the final article.
fierce
physically violent and frightening / strong and powerful / showing strong feeling or energetic activity
Two men were shot during fierce fighting last weekend.
Fierce winds prevented the race from taking place. The expansion plans will face fierce opposition/resistance from environmentalists.

Jan 1

distort
to change something from its usual, original, natural, or intended meaning, condition, or shape. Another one is the misinformation effect, a memory bias in which we subconsciously incorporate new information to distort memory of the original event.
My original statement has been completely distorted by the media.
impede
to make it more difficult for something to happen or more difficult for someone to do something.
Together, the cumulative implications of these biases impede our ability to form the accurate and complete picture needed for effective reflection. Although he’s shy, it certainly hasn’t impeded his career in any way.
disposal
he act of getting rid of something, especially by throwing it away.
We are all investors; the currency at our disposal is time and energy.
